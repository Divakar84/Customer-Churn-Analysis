# 📊 HR Analytics: Employee Attrition Prediction

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations as it impacts productivity and increases hiring costs. This project focuses on analyzing HR data to identify key factors influencing employee attrition and building a machine learning model to predict future attrition.

The project uses data analytics, visualization, and machine learning techniques to generate actionable insights for improving employee retention.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze employee data to understand attrition patterns
* Identify key factors contributing to employee turnover
* Build a predictive model for attrition
* Create an interactive dashboard using Power BI
* Provide recommendations to reduce attrition

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

* **Python** (Pandas, NumPy, Seaborn, Matplotlib)
* **Scikit-learn** (Machine Learning Models)
* **Power BI** (Data Visualization Dashboard)
* **SHAP** (Model Explainability)

---

## 📂 Dataset

* IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset (Kaggle)

### Key Features:

* Age, Gender, Department
* Job Role, Job Level
* Monthly Income
* Job Satisfaction, Work-Life Balance
* OverTime
* Attrition (Target Variable)

---

## 🔍 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Key insights from the analysis:

* Higher attrition observed in Sales department
* Employees working overtime are more likely to leave
* Lower salary is associated with higher attrition
* Employees with fewer years at the company show higher attrition
* Job satisfaction plays a significant role in retention

---

## 🤖 Model Building

* Model Used: **Random Forest Classifier**
* Data Split: 80% Training, 20% Testing
* Feature Engineering and Encoding applied

---

## 📊 Model Evaluation

The model was evaluated using:

* Accuracy Score
* Confusion Matrix
* Precision, Recall, F1-score

---

## 📈 Power BI Dashboard

The dashboard includes:

* KPI Cards (Total Employees, Attrition Count, Attrition Rate, Average Salary)
* Attrition by Department and Job Role
* Attrition vs Salary Band
* Attrition vs Overtime
* Attrition vs Job Satisfaction

---

## 🔑 Key Findings

* Overtime is a major factor influencing attrition
* Employees with lower income are more likely to leave
* Early-stage employees have higher turnover rates
* Poor work-life balance leads to higher attrition

---

## 💡 Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime
* Improve salary structure for low-income employees
* Enhance employee engagement programs
* Provide career growth and promotion opportunities
* Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys

---

## 🚀 Future Improvements

* Use advanced models like XGBoost or Gradient Boosting
* Deploy the model using Flask or Streamlit
